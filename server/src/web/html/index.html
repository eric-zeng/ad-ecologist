How much are advertisers paying for your views?

A research study from the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering
Investigators: Eric Zeng, Rachel McAmis, Tadayoshi Kohno, Franziska Roesner

We want to learn about how much advertisers are paying to show you ads, what is in those ads, and how they might be targeting you. To collect this data, we need you to temporarily install our browser extension, and visit a few websites.

Our extension will automatically collect:

  1. The price the advertiser paid to show you the ad
  2. A screenshot of the ad
  3. The website the ad appeared on
  4. Data about web trackers and analytics on the site

Study Procedure

In this study, you will install our browser extension, and then visit 10 specific websites, so that we can collect data on the ads on those websites.

These are legitimate and trustworthy websites, such as businessinsider.com, foodnetwork.com, and speedtest.net. Visiting these websites will not harm you or your computer.

After visiting the 10 websites, we will ask you to fill out a short survey about some of the ads that we collected.

Once all of these tasks have been completed, you can submit your results and receive your compensation via Prolific.

Privacy

Your privacy is very important to us, and we won't ask you to share any data that you don't feel comfortable sharing.

  • We won't start collecting data from a web page until you give us permission.
  • If you feel that the ads are too personal or don't want to share the ad screenshots for some other reason, you can specify which ads you want to exclude at the end of the study.

For additional details about our data collection and user privacy, please see our privacy policy.

This study was determined to be exempt for Human Subjects Regulations by the University of Washington Institutional Review Board (IRB), meaning that it involves no more than minimal risk to you.

If you have any questions about this study, you can contact the researchers at uwadstudy@cs.washington.edu.
