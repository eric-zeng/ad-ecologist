UW Ad Tracker Extension Privacy Policy

The goal of this project is to learn about how much advertisers are paying to show users ads, what is in those ads, and how they might be targeting specific users.

We are committed to protecting the privacy of all users of our extension. We have established this privacy policy to explain what information we collect through this extension and how it is used.

In this policy, "the researchers" and "we" refer to any and all researchers or assistants otherwise involved in this research project. This project involves personnel from the University of Washington.

The researchers are located within the United States, and therefore will transfer, process, and store your information in the United States.

What information do we collect?

The extension gathers information on ads on a list of specific news and media websites. If you allow the extension to collect data on the website, for each ad, the extension collects:

  • The price the advertiser paid to show you the ad. It also collects information on the ads that bid money to show up on the page but did not end up rendering. This information includes the advertiser bidders, how much each bidder bids, and the price of the winning bid.
  • A screenshot of the ad, and its HTML content. Users may opt out of sharing specific ad screenshots if they feel that they are too personal.
  • The URL of the website the ad appeared on.
  • Data about web trackers and analytics on the site. Specifically, we collect web requests made by third parties on the page, and cookies set by third parties.

How do we use this information?

We collect this data to conduct analyses of how advertisers might be targeting individual users, and the behaviors of advertisers in ad auctions.

For Prolific study participants only, we will also use this data in conjunction with demographic data that you provide to us in a separate pre-screening survey and/or the Prolific service. We will use this demographic information (age, gender, ethnicity, zip code, and location) to study how advertisers target specific demographic categories.

These analyses will strictly be used for scientific research; no data will be used for commercial purposes.

What information do we share?

We intend to publish a research paper incorporating the data we collect. The research paper will not publish any individual-level user data. Instead, we will analyze aggregates of ad screenshot types, ad pricing, and tracker/cookie prevalence across different user demographic information provided in a survey from Prolific participants outside the study.

As part of this project, we may share datasets derived from this project with research partners working on topics related to Internet security or privacy. Before sharing, we will evaluate whether further sanitization or aggregation of data is necessary to reduce the likelihood that inferences about identifiable individuals' activities might be made from the published dataset. Because anonymization is an algorithmically complex problem, we cannot promise that it will be flawless or attack-proof.

Additionally, we may also publicly release and publish anonymized information from datasets to further general scientific knowledge. The datasets we may share or publish will not intentionally contain peresonally identifiable information.

Data storage and retention

The extension sends the information listed above into our private research database for this project. The data listed above in this policy is all linked in the database to the user's specific Prolific ID (which they share with us in a survey outside of this extension).

We will retain the dataset for as long as the data remains useful for research topics related to online tracking, privacy, and targeted advertising.

Updating or removing your information

If you request that you no longer want us to retain the information we collected in this extension, we will delete it since we associate all the data with your Prolific ID. See contact information below if you want to request this.

Security

We employ industry standard security measures to protect the loss, misuse, and alteration of the information under our control by using appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk. Although we make good faith efforts to store information collected by us in a secure operating environment, we cannot guarantee complete security.

Contacting us

If you have any questions about our privacy and data protection practices, you can contact the researchers at: uwadstudy@cs.washington.edu

Changes to Our Policies

This extension's privacy policy may change from time to time. However, any revised privacy policy will be consistent with the purpose of this research project. If we make any substantive changes to our policies, we will post notice of changes on this page.
